# Acaddie — User Instructions
### Short Game Advisor

---

## Quick Reference

1. **Enter distance** to the pin in yards
2. **Tap your lie** — where the ball is sitting
3. **Tap lie detail** if a sub-menu appears (sand type, rough grain, water depth)
4. **Tap slope/stance** — the ground your feet are on
5. **Tap obstacle** if something is between you and the pin (default is None)
6. **Tap GET ADVICE** — button activates once all required fields are filled
7. Read your **shot name, club, and numbered instructions**
8. Check **Slope Adjustment** card if it appears
9. Check **Heads Up** warning card if it appears
10. Tap **← Edit** to go back and change inputs, or **↺ NEW SHOT** to start fresh

---

## Full Walkthrough

### Overview

Acaddie is a short game advisor that works like a caddie. You describe the situation — distance, lie, slope, and obstacles — and it tells you exactly what shot to play, which club to use, and how to execute it in plain language. No golf jargon, no shot names without explanation.

---

### Step 1 — Distance to Green

Tap the distance field and type the number of yards to the **pin** (not the edge of the green). Most golfers read this directly from their rangefinder or GPS watch.

- Minimum: 1 yard
- Maximum: 100 yards
- A small green dot next to the label confirms the field is filled

> **Tip:** Acaddie is designed for short game situations — shots from around and just off the green. For full approach shots over 100 yards, use your normal club yardage system.

---

### Step 2 — Lie

Tap the tile that best describes where your ball is sitting. There are six options:

| Tile | When to use |
|---|---|
| 🌿 Fairway / Fringe | Ball on short grass, fairway cut, or the fringe just off the green |
| 🌾 Light Rough | Ball in rough but sitting up — you can see most of the ball |
| 🌳 Deep Rough | Ball buried or mostly hidden in thick grass |
| 🏖️ Sand / Bunker | Ball in a bunker or sand trap |
| 💧 Shallow Water | Ball is in water but still playable (less than 2 inches deep) |
| 🪨 Hardpan / Bare | Ball on hard dry ground, cart path edge, or bare dirt with no grass |

A small green dot next to the label confirms a lie is selected.

---

### Step 3 — Lie Detail (Contextual)

After selecting certain lies, a sub-menu appears automatically with additional detail. This is required for the advice engine to give you the right shot.

#### Sand / Bunker detail:
| Option | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ Clean / Sitting up | Normal bunker shot — ball is resting on top of the sand |
| 🔽 ⅓ Buried | Ball has sunk about one-third into the sand |
| 🍳 Fried Egg | Ball has plugged and left a crater around it — looks like a fried egg |
| ❌ Fully Buried | Ball is completely under the sand surface |
| 🔒 Deep Plug | Ball is deeply embedded, often against the bank of the bunker |
| 🏌️ Near Edge (chip out) | Ball is close to the lip — a blast is not necessary, a chip will do |

#### Light / Deep Rough detail:
| Option | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ➡️ With Grain | Grass is growing away from you, toward the green — club slides through more easily |
| ⬅️ Against Grain | Grass is growing toward you — the club will be grabbed and slowed |
| ⬇️ Vertical / Matted | Grass is compressed straight down, or the ball is sitting in a nest |

#### Shallow Water detail:
| Option | Meaning |
|---|---|
| 💧 ⅓ Submerged | Ball is one-third under water — top two-thirds visible |
| 🌊 ⅔ Submerged | Ball is mostly underwater — only a small part visible |
| 🏊 Fully Under (<2") | Ball is completely submerged but less than 2 inches deep |

> **Note:** If the water is more than 2 inches deep, take a penalty drop. Acaddie does not advise shots from deep water.

---

### Step 4 — Slope & Stance

Tap the tile that describes the slope of the ground where you are standing, not the slope of the green.

| Tile | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ➖ Flat | Ground is level — normal stance |
| ⬆️ Uphill | You are hitting from a slope that goes up toward the target |
| ⬇️ Downhill | You are hitting from a slope that goes down toward the target |
| ↖️ Ball Above Feet | The ball is higher than your feet — sidehill lie |
| ↘️ Ball Below Feet | The ball is lower than your feet — sidehill lie |

A small green dot next to the label confirms a slope is selected.

> **Tip:** When in doubt between Uphill and Downhill, think about which way your shoulders naturally tilt when you stand over the ball. Uphill = right shoulder lower (for right-handed golfers). Downhill = left shoulder lower.

---

### Step 5 — Obstacles

Tap if anything is between your ball and the pin. Default is **None** (pre-selected).

| Tile | Meaning |
|---|---|
| ✅ None | Clear path to the green — any shot type is possible |
| ☠️ Hazard | A bunker, pond, or rough strip between you and the pin — ball must fly over it |
| 🌲 Tree / Low Ceiling | A tree branch, fence, or low obstacle — ball must stay low |

> **Important:** Selecting Hazard or Tree will eliminate low-running shots from the advice. Acaddie will only suggest shots with enough height or the right trajectory to clear the obstacle.

---

### Step 6 — Get Advice

Once Distance, Lie, and Slope are all filled (green dots lit), the **GET ADVICE** button activates. Tap it to see your advice.

---

### Reading the Output Screen

The output screen has up to four sections:

#### Situation Bar
A one-line summary at the top confirms what you entered — distance, lie, slope, and obstacle. Check this before reading the advice to make sure everything was entered correctly.

#### Recommended Shot
- **Shot name** — the name of the technique in large text
- **Club** — the recommended club shown as both a generic name and loft in degrees, e.g. *Sand Wedge (SW · 56°)*. If two options are shown, the first is preferred.

#### How To Play It
Numbered step-by-step instructions in plain English. Read all steps before playing the shot. Key setup points come first, swing keys come last.

#### ⛰ Slope Adjustment *(appears when slope is not Flat)*
Specific adjustments for your stance, aim, and club choice based on the slope you selected. Always apply these on top of the main instructions.

#### ⚠ Heads Up *(appears when relevant)*
A warning about what to expect from this shot — unusual ball flight, extra roll, high risk, or special technique notes. Read this before committing to the shot.

---

### Navigation

| Button | Action |
|---|---|
| ← Edit | Goes back to the input screen with all your selections preserved — change one thing and get new advice |
| ↺ NEW SHOT | Clears everything and starts fresh for the next shot |

---

### Tips for Best Results

- **Be honest about the lie.** The single biggest factor in shot selection is where the ball is sitting. When in doubt, choose the worse lie — it is better to be over-prepared than surprised.
- **Grain matters in rough.** Against-grain rough is significantly harder than with-grain. Acaddie adjusts the club recommendation to account for lost distance.
- **Sand lie detail is critical.** A clean blast and a buried lie are completely different shots. Take two seconds to look at the ball before tapping.
- **Slope adjustment stacks on top.** The main instructions give you the base shot. The slope card gives you the modification. Apply both.
- **Hazard selection removes options.** If you have a clear path but select Hazard by mistake, you may get a more conservative shot than needed. Always check the situation bar on the output screen.
