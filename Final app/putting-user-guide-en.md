# Putting Calculator — User Guide
**Version 6 · English**

---

## What Is This App?

The Putting Calculator is a golf green-reading tool that runs on your smartphone. It uses your phone's built-in motion sensors to measure the slope of the green, then tells you two things:

- **Where to aim** — how far left or right of the hole to aim your putt
- **How hard to hit** — whether to hit softer or firmer than normal due to uphill or downhill slope

It is designed to be used on the green, before each putt, as a caddie-style aid.

## First-Time Setup

### Step 1 — Enable Motion Sensors
When you open the app for the first time, you will see the **Enable Motion Sensors** screen. Tap the button and allow access when your phone asks for permission.

> On iPhone: if you previously denied access, go to **Settings → Safari → Motion & Orientation Access** and turn it ON, then reload the app.

### Step 2 — Rotate to Landscape
The app only works in **landscape orientation** (phone held sideways). If your phone is upright, you will see a rotate screen. Turn your phone sideways with the **home button on the right**.

### Step 3 — Calibrate the Sensor
Calibration corrects for any tilt in your phone caused by a case, camera bump, or uneven surface.

1. Find a flat, level surface (a table or bench works well)
2. **Place your phone flat on the surface, screen facing up** — do not hold it
3. Tap **Calibrate**
4. Keep your hands away from the phone — a countdown from 8 will begin once the phone is stable
5. When the countdown reaches zero, calibration is saved automatically

> If you have calibrated before, your previous calibration is shown with its date. You can tap **Skip** to use it again, or recalibrate at any time.

Calibration is saved on your phone and does not need to be repeated unless you change your phone case or notice readings seem off.

## Using the App on the Green

### The Main Screen
The app is divided into three sections:

- **Left** — set your putt length and green speed
- **Centre** — measure the slope
- **Right** — see your aim and pace results

---

### Step 1 — Set Your Putt Length
On the left panel, set the distance of your putt in **paces** (one pace ≈ 3 feet).

- Tap **−** or **+** to adjust in 0.5 pace steps
- Or **tap the number directly** to type in a value

> Tip: Walk from your ball to the hole and count your paces.

---

### Step 2 — Set Green Speed (Stimp)
Below the length, set the **Stimp** value — this is the speed rating of the greens, usually displayed on a board near the clubhouse.

- Typical values: **9–11** for most courses, up to **13–14** for tournament conditions
- Adjust with **−** or **+**, or tap the number to type it in
- Default is **10.0**

---

### Step 3 — Measure the Slope

#### How to Place Your Phone
1. Stand beside your ball, facing the hole
2. **Place your phone flat on the green, screen facing up**, in landscape orientation
3. Position the phone so:
   - The **top edge** points toward the **hole** 🚩
   - The **bottom edge** points toward your **ball** ⚪
   - The phone is **perpendicular** to the ball-hole line
   - Use the flag and ball icons on the centre panel as a guide

#### Reading the Slope
1. Tap **Read Slope** on the left panel
2. The centre panel shows live readings:
   - **Side Slope %** — how much the green tilts left or right
   - **Up / Down %** — how much the green slopes uphill or downhill toward the hole
3. Wait for the numbers to settle
4. Tap **Lock ✓** to capture the reading

#### Taking a Second Reading (Optional)
For more accuracy, especially on tricky greens:
1. After locking, tap **New Reading** to take a second measurement
2. Tap **Avg ⌀** to average both readings together
3. This gives a more reliable slope measurement

#### Reset
Tap **Reset** to clear all readings and start again.

---

### Step 4 — Manual Entry (Optional)
If you prefer to enter the slope yourself (e.g. from your own green-reading system):

1. Tap **✏ Manual** at the bottom of the left panel
2. Enter the side slope %, break direction (L or R), and up/down slope if applicable
3. Results update automatically

---

### Step 5 — Read Your Results

Once a slope is locked (or manual entry is active), the right panel shows:

#### Aim
- The **arrow** (← or →) shows which direction to aim
- The **distance** (e.g. `8.5"` or `1' 2"`) tells you how far left or right of the hole centre to aim
- The **cups** value (e.g. `2.0 cups`) gives the same distance in golf cup widths — some golfers find this easier to visualise on the green
- If the green is flat with no break, a **↑ Straight** result is shown

#### Pace
- The **percentage** (e.g. `85%`) tells you how hard to hit relative to a flat putt
  - **100%** = normal pace for a flat putt
  - **Below 100%** = hit softer (downhill — gravity helps)
  - **Above 100%** = hit harder (uphill — gravity resists)
- The **effective distance** (e.g. `≈ 3.5 paces`) tells you the distance you should *feel* you are hitting to — this accounts for the effect of gravity on the ball

## Tips for Best Results

- **Calibrate on a level surface** before your round, not on the green itself
- **Place the phone flat on the green** and wait for the numbers to settle before locking
- **Take two readings** and average them on important putts
- The **centre alignment guide** (🚩 line ⚪) helps you position the phone correctly — the flag should point toward the hole

### Where to Measure on the Green

The slope closest to the hole has the greatest influence on your putt. As the ball slows down near the hole, its direction is most easily changed by gravity. For this reason, **measure the slope at the final third of the putt** — the section nearest the hole — rather than at your ball or midway.

To find the right spot: divide the distance from your ball to the hole into three equal sections. Walk to the boundary between the middle third and the final third (two-thirds of the way to the hole), and take your slope reading there.

### Long Putts With Two Slopes

If the putt is long and you suspect the green changes slope partway — for example, the first half is uphill and the second half breaks left — treat it as two separate putts:

1. Take a reading at the **final third** (closest to the hole) as described above — lock it
2. Tap **New Reading** and take a second reading at the **midpoint** of the putt
3. Tap **Avg ⌀** to average the two readings
4. Use the averaged result as your aim and pace guide
